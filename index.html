Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a renowned Bangladeshi economist and Nobel Laureate, served as Chief Adviser during a transitional period in Bangladesh's history. Born in 1940 in Chittagong, he is celebrated for founding the Grameen Bank and pioneering microfinance, transforming the lives of millions in poverty. As Chief Adviser of the caretaker government in 2007, Dr. Yunus faced the critical task of stabilizing the nation amidst political turmoil, ensuring free and fair elections, and upholding democratic principles.
